Mapping Forensic Anthropology Research

This project will produce an interactive website for exploring the sample data used in forensic anthropological biological profile methods (as per the American Board of Forensic Anthropology reading list). The aim is to provide a resource for individuals looking to understand which skeletal collections are used in research, in which types of research, and how the use of skeletal collections for research has changed over time. This is meant to provide a resource for understanding how currently used and generally accepted forensic anthropological research uses skeletal collections to represent the greater population and whether they actually do so. 

Users will be able to search through maps to look at skeletal collections (and learn more about them). But they will also be able to look through and click on research articles to visually see on the map where each articleâ€™s sample data is sourced (i.e., which skeletal collections). Users can also look at the sources of data over time and by research type.
